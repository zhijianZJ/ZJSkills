# Employment Diagnosis

## Intake without a generic questionnaire

First extract everything already known. A useful diagnosis usually needs:

- Target outcome and desired transition period.
- Current and previous roles, industries, and repeated responsibilities.
- Two or three concrete projects, problems, or measurable results.
- Education or credentials only where they affect entry requirements.
- Evidence in coding, data, product, operations, sales, delivery, research, or industry work.
- Actual AI use, projects, artifacts, and observed difficulties.
- Region, language, work arrangement, time, and important constraints.
- Compensation expectation only when role or company matching requires it.

Ask for missing information in no more than two focused turns. Prefer prompts such as:

> 请介绍你最近几年的实际工作：反复负责什么、解决过什么问题、最拿得出手的两个结果是什么，以及你希望在哪个城市或行业发展。

Follow with only the missing facts that could change the recommendation. Accept narrative answers; do not require a form.

## Translate experience into career assets

For each important experience, use:

observed work → problem solved → result → demonstrated asset → possible AI value → missing evidence

Classify conclusions:

- Demonstrated asset: supported by work, artifact, result, responsibility, or repeated behavior.
- Transfer hypothesis: a plausible use of that asset in an AI role.
- Missing evidence: the task, artifact, or market feedback still needed.

Do not infer capability from title, employer, degree, years, confidence, interest, tool familiarity, or course completion alone.

## Match roles

Score no hidden percentage. Compare candidates on:

1. Desired work result.
2. Reuse of demonstrated assets.
3. Size and difficulty of must-close gaps.
4. Evidence the user can realistically build.
5. Region and company demand when current evidence is available.
6. Time, language, financial, and personal constraints.
7. Response to a relevant minimum experience task.

Recommend one primary role and at most one backup. Use one judgment:

- Strong current match.
- Credible transition with defined gaps.
- Worth testing before committing.
- Not recommended yet.
- Insufficient evidence.

State why nearby roles were not selected. Do not recommend several parallel careers to appear comprehensive.

## Match companies

First match company type, stage, and work environment. Consider:

- Need for structure versus tolerance for ambiguity.
- Preference for specialization versus broad ownership.
- Customer-facing versus internal product work.
- Existing industry advantage.
- Delivery pressure, travel, language, and location.
- Evidence threshold likely required by the employer.

For named companies or current openings, determine region and target period, then research current attributable sources. Prefer employer career pages and direct postings. Require explicit evidence that the role or team performs AI-related work; generic software, e-commerce, data, or digital responsibilities are not enough. Report source date, role, region, why it matches, and sample limitation. Label useful non-AI openings as adjacent references rather than target roles. If current research is unavailable, provide company types and search criteria instead of stale names.

## Build the gap diagnosis

Separate:

- Must close: absence would block credible candidacy or delivery.
- Evidence gap: capability may exist but is not demonstrated.
- Useful enhancement: improves competitiveness but is not an entry gate.
- Existing strength: should be highlighted in projects, resume, and interviews.

Prioritize gaps by target-role importance, not by the number of available courses.

## Market and outcome boundaries

For salary, hiring trend, competition, named-company demand, or market-window claims, set region, role level, and time window before research. Define whether figures mean base pay or total compensation and report sample size and limitation.

Never promise employment or imply that completing a route makes the user job-ready without external evidence. Treat projects, interviews, recruiter feedback, and real work as progressively stronger validation.

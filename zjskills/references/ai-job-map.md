# AI Job Map

Use this map as a starting taxonomy, not an exhaustive list. Prefer AI plus the user's demonstrated industry or functional experience when that creates a credible advantage.

## AI application and engineering

### AI application or Agent engineer

- Work: build, integrate, test, debug, deploy, and monitor model- and tool-based applications.
- Strong evidence: independently changes requirements, diagnoses failures, tests behavior, and delivers a working application.
- Common gap: copied demos without software fundamentals or deployment evidence.
- Company types: AI application startups, enterprise software, model platforms, automation vendors, internal AI engineering teams.

### Data or machine-learning engineer

- Work: data pipelines, features, training systems, evaluation, production data quality, and model operations.
- Strong evidence: reproducible data or ML pipelines with measured results.
- Common gap: notebook-only experiments without production engineering.

### Algorithm, research, or model engineer

- Work: model methods, experiments, training, evaluation, and research or optimization.
- Strong evidence: mathematics, programming, experiment design, paper reproduction, and rigorous evaluation.
- Gate: do not recommend as an easy transition based only on interest, salary, or course exposure.

### AI infrastructure and inference engineer

- Work: distributed systems, GPU utilization, inference serving, performance, reliability, and observability.
- Strong evidence: systems engineering, Linux, networking, profiling, deployment, or performance optimization.
- Gate: require credible software or systems foundations.

## AI product and solution roles

### AI product manager

- Work: translate user and business problems into testable AI product decisions.
- Strong evidence: discovery, prioritization, prototypes, capability boundaries, failure cases, metrics, and acceptance criteria.
- Common gap: feature ideas without user evidence or model-behavior evaluation.
- Company types: AI-native products, SaaS, model platforms, enterprise digital products, traditional companies building AI products.

### AI solution consultant or presales

- Work: understand customer problems, shape an AI solution, demonstrate value, manage boundaries, and support commercial decisions.
- Strong evidence: customer discovery, industry knowledge, solution design, communication, and commercial judgment.
- Common gap: presentation skill without technical understanding or delivery evidence.
- Company types: model platforms, cloud vendors, enterprise software, consulting firms, system integrators, industry AI vendors.

### AI implementation, delivery, or project role

- Work: turn an agreed AI solution into adopted workflows, coordinate stakeholders, validate results, and manage risk.
- Strong evidence: complex project delivery, process mapping, change management, acceptance, and measurable adoption.

## AI business roles

### AI operations, content, or growth

- Work: content, audience, campaigns, user operations, growth loops, and AI-enabled production systems.
- Strong evidence: measurable outcomes and an explanation of AI's causal contribution.
- Common gap: output volume without audience or business results.

### AI sales, business development, or customer success

- Work: identify demand, explain value, develop accounts, support adoption, and expand outcomes.
- Strong evidence: customer results, pipeline or account performance, complex communication, and industry context.
- Common gap: generic sales experience without AI solution literacy.

## AI plus an existing domain

Examples include AI plus architecture, education, manufacturing, finance, healthcare, retail, human resources, legal operations, or supply chain.

Treat domain experience as an advantage only when the user can name real problems, workflows, constraints, stakeholders, and results. Likely roles include industry product, solution consulting, implementation, operations, customer success, or internal transformation.

## Workplace AI capability

Using AI for analysis, writing, automation, research, or coordination may strengthen an existing role. Do not automatically present it as a standalone occupation. It can become transition evidence when the user shows repeatable quality, time, cost, risk, or collaboration improvement.

## Company environment matching

Match environment as well as role:

- Large platform or established company: clearer specialization, stronger process, higher entry evidence.
- AI-native startup: broader ownership, speed, ambiguity, and delivery pressure.
- Consulting or system integration: customer communication, industry knowledge, solution design, and delivery.
- Enterprise software or SaaS: product, implementation, customer success, and workflow knowledge.
- Traditional-company AI team: domain depth, internal coordination, adoption, and transformation.

Name companies only after checking current hiring in the user's region. Verify that the role exists, the posting is current, the role or team contains explicit and verifiable AI work, and the requirements resemble the recommended evidence. Do not infer AI demand from brand reputation, generic digital work, or the employer's unrelated AI activity. If a posting is useful only as adjacent evidence, label it `邻近岗位参考` and do not include it in the target AI-company list.
